> [!info]

## RfAW Reference: ACME-RfAW-2024-001

| Clause | Content |
|--------|---------|
| **Title and reference** | Customer Onboarding Modernisation — Request for Architecture Work · ACME-RfAW-2024-001 · Version 1.0 · 2024-09-15 |
| **Requesting organisation** | Customer Division, ACME Corp. Sponsoring executive: Chief Customer Officer (Sarah Chen). Requesting unit: Enterprise Architecture Practice, on behalf of the Customer Division Programme Board. |
| **Business motivation** | The Customer Onboarding process is ACME's primary point of customer acquisition. The current process takes an average of **11 working days** (baseline: Q2 2024 Operations Report) due to manual identity verification across four disconnected systems. This delay results in a 23% abandonment rate at the onboarding stage (ACME CX Research Q3 2024). Additionally, the core CRM module used in the onboarding workflow reaches vendor end-of-life in Q4 2025, removing security patch support. Regulatory guidance on identity verification (issued Q1 2024) requires digital identity verification to meet a higher assurance standard than the current manual process can deliver. This architecture work is mandated by: (1) the 2024–2027 Corporate Strategy (§3.2 — Customer Acquisition Cost reduction), (2) the Technology Risk Register (Item TR-2024-017 — EoL CRM), and (3) Legal & Compliance directive LC-2024-004. |
| **Business and technical scope** | **In scope:** The end-to-end Customer Onboarding process from initial customer application through identity verification, data capture, risk scoring, and account activation. Systems in scope: Legacy CRM (onboarding module only), Identity Verification Service, Document Capture Service, Customer Master Data system, Notification Service. Organisational units in scope: Customer Operations, IT Operations, Information Security, Legal & Compliance. **Explicitly out of scope:** HR and Finance ERP systems; in-store and branch onboarding channels (separate programme); account management and servicing capabilities post-activation. |
| **Constraints** | **C-01 Budget:** Total programme architecture and delivery budget not to exceed €12M capex over 3 years. Architecture work (Phases A–G) to consume ≤8% of total budget. `[proven]` / one-way door. **C-02 Timeline:** Phase A Architecture Vision and Statement of Architecture Work to be completed by 2025-01-31. Phase B Business Architecture by 2025-04-30. **C-03 Technology platform:** All solution components must deploy on the ACME approved cloud platform (as per Technology Standards Register v4.2); exceptions require Architecture Board approval. `[proven]` / two-way door. **C-04 Regulatory:** Identity verification must meet the assurance level required by the applicable data-protection and identity regulations. Data residency must comply with Principle D-02. `[proven]` / one-way door. **C-05 Existing commitments:** Legacy CRM decommission must align with the vendor EoL date (Q4 2025); any interim architecture must account for this hard deadline. one-way door. |
| **Deliverables required** | Phase A: Statement of Architecture Work, Architecture Vision, Stakeholder Map, Communications Plan. Phase B: Business Capabilities Catalog, Gap Analysis, Value Stream/Capability Matrix. Phase C (Data): Data Architecture Description Document, Data Entity Catalog, Data Security Diagram. Phase C (Application): Application Architecture ADD, Interface Catalog. Phase D: Technology Architecture ADD, Technology Standards confirmation. Phase E/F: Migration Plan and Transition Architecture. Phase G: Architecture Contract (Design & Development), Compliance Assessment. All deliverables to follow ACME's Architecture Repository standards (ACME AR-GUIDE-2024). |
| **Acceptance criteria** | 1. Architecture Vision signed by Chief Customer Officer before Phase B begins. 2. Architecture Principles set (ACME-ARCH-PRIN-2024) approved by Architecture Board before Phase B sign-off. 3. Phase B Business Architecture reviewed and approved by Architecture Board. 4. All Critical findings from Phase G Compliance Assessment resolved before any component enters production. 5. CRM decommission plan incorporated in Phase F Migration Plan before Q3 2025 board submission. 6. Phase G Architecture Contract signed by Architecture Sponsor and Lead Architect for each solution delivery workstream. |
| **Risks and assumptions** | **Risk R-01:** Identity verification SaaS provider selection may take longer than planned if shortlisted providers fail regulatory compliance checks — mitigated by early-stage market engagement and ADR-based decision gate. `[informed estimate]` **Risk R-02:** Legacy CRM vendor may cease security patches earlier than the stated Q4 2025 EoL — mitigated by monitoring vendor communications and Phase F interim architecture option. `[informed estimate]` **Assumption A-01:** The applicable data-protection regime does not change materially during the programme. If it changes, Principle D-02 and the Data Security Diagram must be revised. **Assumption A-02:** The ACME approved cloud platform is capable of hosting all candidate components without requiring a platform exception; this assumption must be validated in Phase D. |
| **Approvals** | **Architecture Sponsor:** Chief Customer Officer (Sarah Chen) — _signature required_ — Date: ____________ **EA Practice Lead / Lead Architect:** Head of Enterprise Architecture (Marcus Webb) — _signature required_ — Date: ____________ · Note: this RfAW has no governance standing until both signatures are in place. |

| Confidence | Reversibility | Owner | Review trigger |
|------------|---------------|-------|----------------|
| `[informed estimate]` — scope and constraints are drafted; final sign-off pending stakeholder review | two-way door at document level (revisions before signature are low-cost); one-way door once signed (scope changes require a formal RfAW amendment) | Head of Enterprise Architecture (Marcus Webb) | Re-issue if: programme budget changes >15%, regulatory requirement changes, or CRM EoL date moves |

**Broad Responsibility:** This programme will reprocess the identity data of all existing ACME customers during migration and capture identity data for all future customers. Any architecture decision affecting identity data handling, consent, or residency has regulatory and societal implications beyond ACME's direct interests — the customers whose data is processed are the primary beneficiaries of good decisions and the primary victims of poor ones. `[proven]`
